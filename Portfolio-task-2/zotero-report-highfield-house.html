    • Very old photo of Lumsden’s Draper shop. Church can be seen on the left. A man standing out the front. Blurred edge around the image.

      1. A man stands by a gate leading into the church, taken between 1878 and 1920 which was the period the store was under the name of Lumsden.

      5. edges of the photo are faded, so only the two buildings can be seen
      6. Eye is drawn to the church and the man standing by the fence
      7. Proves the heritage of the Draper/Grocer store

    Notes:

    • Black and white photo, quite faded around the top. Sibley’s store. Two open top vehicles out the front with people sitting in them, others standing on the footpath.

      1. A large group of people gathered outside Sibley’s Drapery and Grocery Store. The store was owned by Sibleys between 1921 and 1928, so must have been taken in that period.
      2. Staged
      3. There are two open topped vehicles parked facing each other on the road at the front of the building. The roof is not very clear due to deterioration of the photo.The Sibley sign is very prominent.
      4. Both vehicles have a number of passengers, and there is a group of people standing next to the verandah posts. Facial expressions hard to judge.
      5. Background is blurred from photo deterioration
      6. The eye is drawn to the woman in the long, light coloured dress as everyone else has quite dark clothing. The arrangement of the vehicles seems to have been deliberate to frame the edge of the photo.
      7. Types of vehicles and clothing from the period.
